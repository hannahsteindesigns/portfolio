I design user interfaces and develop the front-end code to make those designs a reality.

I take projects from initial sketches through static wireframes and basic prototypes all the way to the live finished product. I'm increasingly passionate about user experience design as a guiding principle for everything I create.

I believe that technology should be above all make life easier. I consider myself an advocate for the user and love the challenge of marrying the best interests of user and business to create a solution that benefits everyone.

My early roots are in print design, and I still sometimes rely on those fundamental design principles and knowledge of how people interact with information on a base level. I'm fascinated with how those principles scale — or don't — to web and mobile interactions as well as the unique problems new technologies face.

In my current position at Hospicelink - StateServ in Birmingham, Alabama, I create and update interfaces for a client-facing web app. Our software allows hospices to order medical equipment and manage patients, and the equipment provider to manage delivery and pickup orders.

My responsibilities include researching and gathering feedback from both internal and external users of our software, identifying inefficiencies, designing intuitive interfaces, and implementing the front-end code to make those designs a reality.
