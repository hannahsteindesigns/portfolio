literary magazine layout & curation

Tower — University of Montevallo

In my final year of school at Montevallo, I was chosen as the Editor-in-Chief of the university's literary magazine. I managed a team of section editors to solicit and select entries from the student body. At the end of the year, I combined these entries into a book that was printed and given to all students. On top of that, I was able to stay within my (somewhat meager) budget.

ad design & menu redesign

Rojo Latin & American Food

I initially created this ad as a t-shirt design for a contest the restaurant held, but when I didn't win they asked me to adapt the design for an ad. (No hard feelings!) That ad ran in the weekly news magazine Weld for nearly two years.

After I designed the ad, we worked together doing routine menu updates along with a redesign of their cocktail menu. It was really immportant to them to have the design be lighter in color to conserve ink when printing, but it still had to fit in with the other existing menus. I created an interactive PDF so they can update their draft beers at will.

Central Research Associates

Central Research Associates came to me to set up a web presence. They had two goals that sometimes conflicted â€” provide information for patients looking to enroll in free trials as well as information for other research organizations and hospitals looking to partner with them. The challenge was to inform the latter without overwhelming the former with unnecessary information.

After we determined trial participants would be the primary audience, I accomplished this by placing a large, attention-grabbing call to action on the homepage that immediately whisks potential participants away to the page relevant to them. Others looking for more in-depth information can follow the links below that or use the navigation. I created icons to categorize their text-heavy pages, grouped like content under linked subheadings, and instituted cross-linking throughout all the informative pages.
